About us

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the national space agency of India, responsible for the planning, execution and management of India’s space research and development programs. Formed in 1969, ISRO operates under the Department of Space (DoS) and is guided by the vision of using space technology for national development and scientific advancement. ISRO was founded with the goal of ensuring that the benefits of space technology reach every citizen. What began as a modest program has now evolved into a globally respected space agency, praised for its innovative approach, low-cost missions, and technological self-reliance.

ISRO’s activities span a wide range of domains, including:

  • Satellite Design and Development —

    building advanced satellites for communication, earth observation, weather forecasting, navigation and scientific research.

  • Launch Vehicle Development —

    creating reliable and cost-effective launch vehicles such as the PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle) and GSLV (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle).

  • Space Science and Planetary Exploration —

    conducting ambitious missions to explore the Moon, Mars, Sun and beyond.

  • Application of Space Technology —

    using space-based systems to support agriculture, disaster management, education, health, environmental monitoring and urban planning.

  • Research & Innovation —

    continuously investing in advanced technology, robotics, propulsion systems and cutting-edge scientific instruments.
