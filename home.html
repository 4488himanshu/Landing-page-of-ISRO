Welcome to ISRO –

Indian Space Research Organisation The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is India’s premier national space agency, dedicated to harnessing space technology for national development and advancing scientific research and exploration. Established in 1969, ISRO has grown into one of the world’s leading space agencies, known for its innovation, cost-effective missions and pioneering achievements.

Our Vision

To harness space technology for national growth while pursuing space science research and planetary exploration to enrich humanity’s understanding of the universe.

Our Mission

  • Design and develop satellites and launch vehicles.
  • Provide reliable launch services for India and international partners.
  • Advance space-based technologies for communication, navigation, earth observation and meteorology.
  • Promote space science and planetary exploration missions.

Key Achievements

  • Satellite Launches

    Over 120 satellites launched for various purposes including communication, remote sensing, navigation, scientific research and national security.

  • PSLV – India’s Workhorse

    Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) has successfully launched missions to Earth orbit, Moon and Mars with a record of high reliability.

  • Chandrayaan Missions

    • Chandrayaan-1 (2008): India’s first lunar mission, discovered water molecules on the Moon.
    • Chandrayaan-2 (2019): Demonstrated India’s capability in lunar orbiter and developed advanced landing technologies.

  • Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission)

    Launched in 2013, ISRO became the first space agency in the world to reach Mars on its first attempt and at a record-low cost.

  • Navigation System – NavIC

    An independent regional navigation satellite system that provides accurate real-time positioning to users in India and surrounding regions.
